Cardboard Box Casserole

Background

This recipe has been passed down for generations. The culinary process is said to have originated in the bustling kitchens of a royal palace, where a fussy royal cat once rejected a solid-gold food bowl in favor of the plain, discarded box it came in. The recipe became a courtly delicacy, known for its rustic charm and its ability to turn even the most regal feline into a blissful lump. Its popularity spread, proving that sometimes the packaging is more appetizing than the contents.
